# Construction Prompts Reference

## Overview

These prompts guide Phase 5 (Draft) - generating proposal content for each section.

---

## Section Generation Master Prompt

### Context
You are generating a proposal section. Follow the template structure while injecting client-specific content.

### Input Required
- Section template
- Client profile
- Company profile
- Strategy package (themes, positioning)
- Assessment package (classification, pricing)

### Generation Guidelines

**Always:**
- Lead with client benefit, not your capability
- Use "you" language (client-focused)
- Thread win themes throughout
- Quantify whenever possible
- Use short sentences (15 words max average)
- One idea per paragraph

**Never:**
- Start with "We are..." or your company name
- Use jargon the client won't understand
- Leave claims unsubstantiated
- Exceed page targets significantly
- Include placeholders without clear labels

## Executive Summary Prompt

### Purpose
Capture the entire proposal in a compelling overview.

### Structure
```
1. THE CHALLENGE (Lead with client pain - quantified)
   [What problem they face, quantified impact]

2. THE SOLUTION (What you'll deliver - outcomes focused)
   [What changes for them, not what you do]

3. WHAT STAYS THE SAME (Reduce fear of change)
   [Systems and processes that won't be disrupted]

4. WHAT CHANGES (The transformation)
   [Specific outcomes they'll experience]

5. THE INVESTMENT (Clear, confident, value-focused)
   [Base amount, timeline, break-even, ROI]

6. WHY YOU (Brief differentiators - 3 max)
   [Specific, provable differentiators]

7. NEXT STEP (Clear call to action)
   [Specific action with timeline]
```

### Writing Guidelines
- Maximum 1-1.5 pages
- Write LAST (after all other sections)
- Most important page in the proposal
- Decision makers may only read this
- Every word must earn its place

### Example Opening
```
BAD: "ABC Company is pleased to submit this proposal for..."
GOOD: "Your team spends 15+ hours every week on manual resume screening.
       That's over $50,000 annually in lost productivity—time your recruiters
       could spend building relationships with candidates."
```

## Understanding of Needs Prompt

### Purpose
Demonstrate you understand their situation.

### Structure
```
1. THEIR CURRENT SITUATION
   [Describe their environment using their language]

2. THE CHALLENGES THEY FACE
   [List specific challenges - ideally from their words]

3. THE IMPACT
   [Quantified business impact of these challenges]

4. WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE
   [Measurable success criteria - things they can hold you to]
```

### Writing Guidelines
- Use language from discovery (their words)
- Show you've done your homework
- Don't just restate the RFP - add insight
- Connect challenges to outcomes you'll deliver
- Make them feel heard and understood

### Example
```
BAD: "The client is experiencing challenges with their processes."
GOOD: "Your recruiters currently review 90% of resumes that will never
       result in a hire. At 200 resumes per week, that's 180 wasted reviews—
       roughly 15 hours your team could spend on qualified candidates."
```

## Technical/Solution Approach Prompt

### Purpose
Explain what you'll deliver and how.

### Structure
```
1. SOLUTION OVERVIEW
   [High-level description of what you'll deliver]

2. KEY COMPONENTS
   [Major components and their purpose]

3. HOW IT WORKS
   [Your methodology/process in accessible terms]

4. INTEGRATION
   [How solution connects to their existing environment]

5. DELIVERABLES
   [Specific outputs they'll receive]
```

### Writing Guidelines
- Focus on outcomes, not activities
- Use their language, not technical jargon
- Connect everything to their requirements
- Show the logic of your approach
- Include visuals (tables, diagrams) where helpful

### Methodology Integration
When referencing your methodology:
- Use accessible language
- Explain the "why" not just the "what"
- Connect methodology steps to their outcomes
- Don't make methodology the hero (client is hero)

## Timeline/Work Plan Prompt

### Purpose
Show how the work will unfold.

### Structure
```
1. PHASE OVERVIEW
   [High-level phases with durations]

2. DETAILED PHASES
   For each phase:
   - Duration
   - Key activities
   - Deliverables
   - Milestone
   - Client involvement required

3. TIMELINE TABLE/VISUAL
   [Easy-to-scan timeline]

4. DEPENDENCIES
   [What you need from them to stay on track]
```

### Writing Guidelines
- Be specific about dates/durations
- Make milestones meaningful (not just "end of phase")
- Show clear progression
- Be realistic (don't overpromise)
- Include client responsibilities

### Timeline Table Template
```
| Week | Phase | Key Activities | Deliverable | Milestone |
|------|-------|----------------|-------------|-----------|
| 1-2  | [Name] | [Activities] | [Output] | [Milestone] |
```

## Investment/Pricing Prompt

### Purpose
Present the business case for the investment.

### Structure
```
1. INVESTMENT SUMMARY
   [Clean, clear total with brief breakdown]

2. WHAT'S INCLUDED
   [Everything they get for the investment]

3. ROI ANALYSIS
   [The business case - why it's worth it]

4. PAYMENT TERMS
   [How and when they pay]

5. SUCCESS ALIGNMENT (if applicable)
   [Success fee structure if included]
```

### Writing Guidelines
- Use "investment" not "cost" or "price"
- Lead with value, not just numbers
- Make ROI irresistible
- Keep payment terms simple
- Never apologize for pricing

### ROI Presentation Template
```
| Your Current Cost | With Our Solution | Your Savings |
|-------------------|-------------------|--------------|
| $[Current] annually | $[Future] annually | $[Savings] annually |

Break-even: Month [X]
Year 1 Return: [X]x your investment
```

## Past Performance Prompt

### Purpose
Prove you've done this before.

### Structure
```
1. CASE STUDY SELECTION
   [Choose 2-3 most relevant from your library]

2. FOR EACH CASE STUDY:
   - Client/Industry (similar to this client)
   - Challenge (similar to this challenge)
   - Solution (similar to this solution)
   - Results (quantified outcomes)
   - Relevance (why this matters for this client)

3. TESTIMONIAL (if available)
   [Quote from happy client]
```

### Writing Guidelines
- Select for relevance, not impressiveness
- Quantify results wherever possible
- Draw explicit connections to this opportunity
- Keep each case study concise (half page max)
- Include testimonials if available

### Case Study Template
```
### [Client/Industry]: [Project Name]

**Their situation:** [Similar to this client's situation]

**What we delivered:** [Solution description]

**Results:**
- [Quantified result 1]
- [Quantified result 2]
- [Quantified result 3]

**Why this matters for you:** [Explicit connection to this opportunity]
```

## Team/Staffing Prompt

### Purpose
Show the right people for the job.

### Structure
```
1. TEAM OVERVIEW
   [How the team is structured]

2. KEY PERSONNEL
   For each key person:
   - Name and title
   - Role on this project
   - Relevant experience (2-3 bullets)
   - Why they're right for this

3. SUPPORT STRUCTURE
   [Additional resources available]
```

### Writing Guidelines
- Lead with role on this project
- Focus on relevant experience
- Keep bios brief and punchy
- Show depth without overwhelming
- Connect experience to their needs

## Risk Management Prompt

### Purpose
Show you've thought about what could go wrong.

### Structure
```
1. RISK ASSESSMENT
   [Key risks identified and rated]

2. MITIGATION STRATEGIES
   [How you'll address each risk]

3. CONTINGENCY PLANS
   [What happens if risks materialize]
```

### Writing Guidelines
- Be honest about real risks
- Don't use boilerplate
- Show thoughtful mitigation
- Demonstrate experience
- Keep it balanced (not fear-mongering)

### Risk Table Template
```
| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| [Specific risk] | [L/M/H] | [L/M/H] | [Specific mitigation] |
```

## Next Steps Prompt

### Purpose
Make it easy to say yes.

### Structure
```
1. IF YOU'D LIKE TO PROCEED
   [Clear, simple steps to move forward]

2. WHAT HAPPENS NEXT
   [First actions after approval]

3. CONTACT
   [Who to reach with questions]
```

### Writing Guidelines
- Remove all friction
- Be specific about actions
- Include timeline
- Make contact easy
- End with confidence, not desperation
