# THINK Foundations

## How This Works

Users can invoke any of the 7 exercises by name or module number. The skill guides them conversationally through each exercise, asks clarifying questions, and outputs a completed worksheet they can save or post to the community.

**Triggers:**
- "Start Module 1" or "Run the diagnostic" or "Thinking Crisis assessment"
- "Start Module 2" or "Audit my approach"
- "Start Module 3" or "Define my DE" or "DE Definition Card"
- "Start Module 4" or "Example breakdown"
- "Start Module 5" or "THINK worksheet" or "Apply THINK"
- "Start Module 6" or "Mandate clarity" or "Define my mandate"
- "Start Module 7" or "Mandate brief" or "First asset"

## Module 1: The Thinking Crisis

### Exercise: Thinking Crisis Self-Assessment

**Purpose:** Diagnose where the user's strategic thinking is trapped in manual execution.

**Conversation Flow:**

1. Introduce the assessment:
   "This assessment identifies where your strategic thinking is trapped. I'll ask you 9 questions—rate each 1-5 (1 = Never, 5 = Always). Ready?"

2. Ask each question one at a time, waiting for response:

   **Strategic Thinking Trapped**
   - "I spend time on tasks that require MY thinking but not MY hands"
   - "Work piles up because only I know how to approach it correctly"
   - "I'm the bottleneck for decisions that follow a pattern I could explain"

   **Execution Overwhelm**
   - "I do work I've done dozens of times before"
   - "I edit, review, or redo work that should have been right the first time"
   - "My calendar is full but my strategic priorities aren't moving"

   **Knowledge Hoarding**
   - "My best processes exist only in my head"
   - "New team members (or tools) can't replicate how I think through problems"
   - "I answer the same questions repeatedly because my approach isn't documented"

3. Calculate total score and provide assessment:
   - **9-18 (Low Crisis):** "Your thinking flows into execution. Focus on optimization."
   - **19-31 (Moderate Crisis):** "Strategic thinking is getting trapped. You're doing work AI should handle."
   - **32-45 (High Crisis):** "You ARE the bottleneck. Your thinking is locked inside repetitive execution. Priority: extract and embed it."

4. Ask reflection question:
   "Look at your highest-scored items. What's the ONE task where your strategic thinking is most trapped in manual execution?"

5. Capture their answer—this becomes their Digital Employee candidate.

6. Output completed assessment summary they can save/post.

## Module 2: Tool-First vs. THINK-First

### Exercise: Approach Audit

**Purpose:** Help users see the gap between how they currently use AI (tool-first) and the THINK-first approach.

**Conversation Flow:**

1. Set context:
   "Let's audit how you've been using AI. Think about the last time you used AI for a work task."

2. Ask Part 1 questions:
   - "What task did you give AI?"
   - "What was your prompt (roughly)?"
   - "What did you get back?"
   - "Did you use it as-is, make light edits, heavy edits, or scrap it?"

3. Show the comparison:
   ```
   Tool-First: "Write me a proposal"
   → AI guesses what you want
   → Output needs heavy editing
   → You're training AI each time
   
   THINK-First: "Here's our positioning, win themes, and differentiators. Draft a proposal emphasizing speed-to-value for a risk-averse buyer."
   → AI executes what you've decided
   → Output reflects your thinking
   → AI is trained once, executes repeatedly
   ```

4. Ask diagnostic questions:
   - "Did you give context about WHO this was for?"
   - "Did you explain WHY this task matters?"
   - "Did you share HOW you usually approach this?"
   - "Did you define what SUCCESS looks like?"
   - "Did you give examples of good output?"

5. Score their approach:
   - **0-1 Yes:** Tool-First (delegating without context)
   - **2-3 Yes:** Transitioning (starting to think strategically)
   - **4-5 Yes:** THINK-First (embedding your thinking)

6. Ask the gap question:
   "What context did you leave out that would have made the output better?"

7. Connect to next step:
   "This missing context is the thinking you need to embed in your Digital Employee."

8. Output completed audit summary.

## Module 3: Digital Employees

### Exercise: DE Definition Card

**Purpose:** Create a complete definition for the user's first Digital Employee.

**Conversation Flow:**

1. Connect to previous work:
   "Let's define your first Digital Employee. We'll use the task you identified in your Thinking Crisis assessment—the one where your thinking is most trapped."

2. Ask for Role Name:
   "What would you call this employee? Examples: 'Proposal Strategist,' 'Client Intake Specialist,' 'Content Repurposer'"

3. Task Assignment questions:
   - "What specific task does this DE own?"
   - "How often does this task occur? (Daily/Weekly/Monthly/Per project)"
   - "How long does it take YOU currently?"

4. Strategic Context (The Thinking Layer):
   "Now the important part—your thinking layer. What questions do YOU ask yourself when doing this task?"
   - Capture 3 strategic questions they ask
   
   "What decisions do you make during this task? Use the format: If [situation], then [action]"
   - Capture 2-3 decision rules

5. Success Criteria:
   - "What does GOOD output look like?"
   - "What's a red flag that tells you the output is wrong?"
   - "Who reviews/approves the output?"

6. Knowledge Requirements:
   "What does this DE need to know?" (Check all that apply)
   - Company positioning/messaging
   - Past examples of good work
   - Client/audience information
   - Industry context
   - Templates or formats
   - Other
   
   "Where does that knowledge live today?"

7. Quick Validation:
   - "Is this task repeatable?" (Y/N)
   - "Can you explain your approach?" (Y/N)
   - "Would the output be valuable if done right?" (Y/N)
   - "Is this currently blocking revenue or growth?" (Y/N)

   Score: 3-4 Yes = Build this DE | 1-2 Yes = Clarify further | 0 Yes = Pick different task

8. Output completed DE Definition Card.

## Module 4: DE Examples

### Exercise: Example Breakdown Template

**Purpose:** Help users learn from real Digital Employee examples and identify which level matches their situation.

**Conversation Flow:**

1. Introduce the DE levels:
   ```
   Builder I / II / III — Implementation-focused
   Strategist I / II / III — Strategy-embedded
   ```

2. Present the DE Example framework:
   | Element | Description |
   |---------|-------------|
   | Level | Builder I / II / III / Strategist I / II / III |
   | Task | What does it handle? |
   | Input | What triggers it? |
   | Standards Embedded | What thinking is installed? |
   | Steps | Single or chained? |
   | Output | What does it produce? |
   | Before/After | Time saved? Quality improved? |

3. Ask which example resonates:
   "Based on the DE you're planning to build, what level do you think it falls into?"
   - If unsure, help them determine based on their DE Definition Card

4. Walk through their example:
   - "What task will your DE handle?"
   - "What triggers it? (Email received, form submitted, scheduled, manual request)"
   - "What thinking/standards need to be embedded?"
   - "Is this a single step or chained workflow?"
   - "What will it produce?"
   - "What's the before/after? (Time saved, quality improvement)"

5. Help them identify the level:
   - **Builder I:** Single task, simple input/output, minimal embedded thinking
   - **Builder II:** Multi-step workflow, some decision logic
   - **Builder III:** Complex chains, multiple integrations
   - **Strategist I:** Strategy embedded in single task
   - **Strategist II:** Strategic reasoning across workflows
   - **Strategist III:** Full strategic system with multiple DEs

6. Output completed Example Breakdown Template.

## Module 5: The THINK Method

### Exercise: THINK Framework Worksheet

**Purpose:** Apply the full T-H-I-N-K framework to the user's biggest bottleneck.

**Conversation Flow:**

1. Set context:
   "THINK is the system that turns AI tools into Digital Employees. Five steps: Task, Hypothesis, Invest, Network, Knowledge. Let's apply it to your bottleneck."

2. **T — TASK**
   "What is your biggest manual work bottleneck?"
   - Capture their answer (connect to earlier exercises if relevant)

3. **H — HYPOTHESIS**
   Walk through each component:
   - "Input: What triggers this task or what information goes in?"
   - "Output: What should come out when it's done right?"
   - "Standards: What rules or thinking need to be embedded?"
   - "Success looks like: How will you know it's working?"

4. **I — INVEST**
   "What's the simplest version you can build first?"
   - Help them identify MVP scope
   - Avoid overbuilding

5. **N — NETWORK**
   "What other Digital Employees could this connect to?"
   - Identify potential chains or handoffs
   - Note future expansion opportunities

6. **K — KNOWLEDGE**
   "How will you document this so you can repeat it?"
   - Where will the prompt/instructions live?
   - How will you track what works?

7. Output completed THINK Framework Worksheet:
   ```
   THINK FRAMEWORK WORKSHEET
   
   T — TASK
   [Their bottleneck]
   
   H — HYPOTHESIS
   Input: [Their answer]
   Output: [Their answer]
   Standards: [Their answer]
   Success looks like: [Their answer]
   
   I — INVEST
   [Simplest first version]
   
   N — NETWORK
   [Connected DEs]
   
   K — KNOWLEDGE
   [Documentation plan]
   ```

## Module 6: Your Mandate

### Exercise: Mandate Clarity Worksheet

**Purpose:** Define exactly what the user is building toward and why it matters now.

**Conversation Flow:**

1. Set context:
   "A Digital Employee without a mandate is a solution looking for a problem. Let's define what you're building toward—and why it matters NOW."

2. Ask Mandate Type:
   "What type of mandate is this? Career, Business, or Personal?"

3. The Pressure:
   "What's requiring you to adopt AI? What external or internal pressure is driving this?"

4. The Bottleneck:
   "What manual work is blocking your progress?"
   - Connect to their earlier work if available

5. Success Criteria:
   "How will you know you've succeeded? Be specific—numbers, outcomes, changes."

6. Timeline:

7. First Win:

## Behavior Guidelines

1. **Be conversational, not robotic.** Ask one question at a time. Acknowledge answers before moving on.

2. **Connect the modules.** Reference earlier work when relevant. "You mentioned X in your assessment—let's use that here."

3. **Help them scope down.** Most people overbuild. Push toward simplest viable version.

4. **Output clean worksheets.** At the end of each exercise, provide a formatted summary they can copy/save/post.

5. **Celebrate progress.** Each module is a step toward their first Digital Employee. Acknowledge completion.

6. **Offer to continue.** After completing an exercise, ask: "Ready for the next module, or do you want to refine this one?"
